The "Inspiration from Polya" section of the SolveIt post draws directly from George Polya’s 1945 book, *How to Solve It*, which outlines a four-step problem-solving framework originally intended for mathematics.

### Polya’s Four-Step Framework
1.  **Understand the Problem:** Identify the core requirements and restate the problem in your own words.
2.  **Devise a Plan:** Break the problem into manageable parts, look for similar problems, or simplify the task.
3.  **Carry Out the Plan:** Execute the steps and verify each one as you go.
4.  **Look Back and Reflect:** Consider alternative solutions and extract lessons learned for future use.

### Relation to Coding and the 'SolveIt' System
The SolveIt system applies Polya’s framework to modern software engineering by emphasizing **iterative, exploratory coding** rather than "vibe coding" or relying on AI to "one-shot" complex tasks.

*   **Granular Iteration:** Instead of writing large blocks of code, the "SolveIt way" involves writing only a few lines at a time. Each snippet is executed immediately to verify its output before moving to the next step.
*   **Fast Feedback Loops:** By working in a notebook-like environment, developers get near-instant feedback. This makes it easier to catch mistakes early and ensures a deep understanding of every line written.
*   **AI as a Partner, Not a Replacement:** SolveIt uses AI to help with specific sub-tasks or to explain confusing concepts. The system is designed to keep the human in control—for example, AI-generated code is not automatically executed, encouraging the user to read and refactor it first.
*   **Shared Context and Dialog Engineering:** The platform ensures the AI sees the same context (notes, plans, and tests) as the human. Because SolveIt dialogs are editable, users can "engineer" the conversation by deleting AI mistakes or dead ends, preventing the model from falling into a loop of repeating its own errors.
*   **Avoiding "Understanding Debt":** The authors argue that letting AI write entire programs creates technical debt and stunts the developer's growth. By following Polya’s steps, developers build a foundation of mastery that allows them to solve harder problems than an AI could handle alone.
